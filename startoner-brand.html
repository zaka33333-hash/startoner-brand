03

Name.

One word. No hyphen. No separation. Name consistency is 60% of the brand-identity job.

Correct
Startoner

One single word. The leading "S" always capitalized in editorial copy. The logo reads as a single element.

Incorrect
Star Toner / Star-Toner / STARTONER

Never with a space, never hyphenated, never in full caps (except inside logos or eyebrow labels). The star-toner.com domain is historical — the brand is Startoner.

Usage rules

  • First mention per page: Startoner. Subsequent mentions: Startoner (never abbreviated as "ST" or similar).
  • In legal/formal contexts: CHICTRATEC S.L. (commercial brand Startoner). Never reversed.
  • In short caps-only titles (eyebrows, badges, all-caps labels): STARTONER is acceptable.
  • Hashtags and handles: #startoner · @startoner. No hyphen, no "official", no "spain".
  • Canonical domain: star-toner.com. Hyphen is historical; preserved for SEO continuity.

Usage rules — third-party trademarks

The marks HP, Canon, Brother, Samsung, Epson, Lexmark, Kyocera, Xerox and OKI are property of their respective owners. Startoner displays them for purely descriptive purposes to indicate technical compatibility, in line with Art. 14 Spanish Trademark Law 17/2001 and the CJEU criterion (case C-228/03 Gillette). They are never visually equated with the Startoner logo: max 60% the size of the adjacent Startoner logo, neutral background, no gradients or effects. The accompanying phrase always includes "compatible with" — never "made by" or "endorsed by".

No emojis in critical interfaces

PNG icons are used in cards, lists, and trust signals. Emojis (📦 🔒 ✅) are banned in checkout, PDP, footer, and transactional emails: they render differently across operating systems and degrade the serious-technical-brand perception. Allowed only in blog body and social media.

14

What Startoner never does.

A brand is defined as much by what it rejects as by what it adopts. This list weighs as much as the color palette and the typography. Every point comes from a concrete finding in the pre-launch audit of April 26, 2026.

"Founded in 1998" applied to the brand.

The Startoner brand launched in 2025. The "1998" figure belongs to the parent group Recycop. Conflating them violates Spanish Unfair Competition Law Art. 5 (deception) and triggers Google Ads Misrepresentation suspensions.

"25 years without a single documented case."

It sounds good, but it's a verifiable record Startoner can't prove — and the internal audit found zero documentation backing it. Removed from homepage, FAQ, llms-txt, and JSON-LD.

Citing fabricated EU regulations.

GDPR (EU) 2016/679 protects personal data, not printer guarantees. Regulation 2017/1369 governs energy labelling. 2018/302 governs geo-blocking. For warranty claims, only: art. 120 RDL 1/2007 (LGDCU) + Directive (EU) 2019/771.

"Monday to Friday 09:00-14:00."

Imposing office hours contradicts the support promise. When a customer buys a cartridge on a Saturday afternoon, they don't want to see "closed". Always replace with "Siempre disponibles" ("Always available") or drop the hours line entirely.

"4.8/5 on certified reviews."

While PDPs still say "Be the first to write a review", a homepage rating destroys credibility in 1 second of scroll. Until Judge.me has 50+ real reviews: use verifiable trust signals (3-year guarantee · ISO 9001 · 30-day returns).

Visually equating our brand to HP / Canon / Brother.

In any listing, schema, or copy, the Startoner logo never appears at the same size or treatment as OEM manufacturer marks. OEM marks are cited only "for descriptive purposes to indicate compatibility" (Spanish Trademark Law Art. 14).

Star Toner · Star-Toner · STARTONER in editorial copy.

One single word, one capital "S": Startoner. The star-toner.com domain is historical and isn't changed for SEO reasons, but the written brand is always Startoner. Full caps only in eyebrows / badges.

Unverifiable adjectives.

Removed: "premium", "superior quality", "innovative", "the best", "leader in". Replace with numbers, certifications, or legal citations. If it has no source, it doesn't ship.

Refunds below the legal deadline.

Saying "we refund in 7 business days after quality validation" violates Spanish LGDCU art. 76 (maximum 14 calendar days from the cancellation notice). Creates LCD Art. 5 liability + LGDCU Art. 105 (automatic 12-month extension).

Italic on H1 / headlines.

Manrope 800 + CMYK gradient already does all the differentiation work. Adding font-style: italic on top of the gradient ruins legibility and visually saturates. Rule: typographic emphasis comes from color, never from slant. Italic is allowed only for real italics (literal quotes, book titles, foreign-language words).

Em-dashes (—) outside the logo.

The em-dash betrays an unreviewed draft. Always replaceable by comma, semicolon, or colon. No editorial Startoner copy should carry one. Exception: the logo wordmark when used in compositions.

Glass-morphism + decorative drop-shadows.

No backdrop-filter: blur on CTAs, no semi-translucent pseudo-iOS layers. Shadows are functional (card elevation on hover); decorative ones are dropped. The visual signature is the radial CMYK gradients + the silver-rotating-glow.

Stock photography of generic toner.

Product imagery must be real photos from the Los Barrios warehouse, with our packaging and our seals. Generic placeholders (any https://images.unsplash.com/* with a pink cartridge floating in space) are forbidden.

"Our factory" / "we manufacture" citing Recycop.

Recycop is a wholesale distributor + technical service + MPS — not a manufacturer. Per recycop.ma: it acts as importer/distributor of international brands, with no in-house industrial line. Any copy claiming "our factory" or "we produce" while citing Recycop is LCD Art. 5. Correct phrasing: "Catalog and audited manufacturer network through Grupo Recycop, specialized distributor since 1998."

Implying Recycop has Spanish operations.

Recycop is Moroccan (Tangier, .ma TLD, French as base language). Its B2C store ships only to Morocco. The Spanish operation is 100% CHICTRATEC S.L. from Cádiz. Correct phrasing: "Startoner is operated by CHICTRATEC S.L. from Los Barrios (Cádiz); its industrial backbone is Grupo Recycop (Tangier, 1998)." Never: "Recycop Spain" or "Recycop HQ in Cádiz".

Search vocabulary.

The search modal is a premium component as codified as the hero. This section locks in the visual language of the Apex modal (`apex-search-v3.css` + `apex-search-v3.js`) — from the input prefix icon to the per-resource fallback icons. If the search doesn't feel like an editorial piece, it isn't Startoner.

Modal header

  • Dark glass panel: rgba(12,12,16,0.84) + backdrop-filter: blur(40px) saturate(160%).
  • Inner border: 1px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.08) + inset highlight rgba(255,255,255,0.06).
  • Ambient CMYK backdrop: cyan top-left + magenta bottom-right + yellow bottom-center, all at 5-12% opacity.
  • Border-radius: 28px desktop, 0 mobile (full-screen).

Main input

  • Cyan magnifying-glass icon on the left, animates to color: rgb(var(--apex-cyan)) + scale(1.05) on focus.
  • Typography: Manrope 800, size clamp(28px, 4vw, 40px), letter-spacing -0.025em.
  • Cyan caret: caret-color: rgb(var(--apex-cyan)).
  • NO border, NO outline. border:none!important;outline:none!important. The bottom gradient line is the signature.

Bottom line — CMYK gradient

  • Idle: horizontal gradient cyan→mint→magenta at 45% opacity. Signals "you're in a serious search".
  • Focus: 100% opacity, cyan halo around it (box-shadow on the container).
  • Loading: animated progress bar overlay, animation: pulseLine 1.2s, only visible when there's a query.

Keyboard hints

  • Top-right of the modal, next to the close button.
  • Pills: ↵ go · esc close in JetBrains Mono 10px, background rgba(255,255,255,0.04).
  • Activate the premium "command palette" feel — Linear, Raycast, Stripe do this.
  • Hidden on mobile (doesn't apply to touch).

Eyebrow pulse-dot on headers

  • Every section label inside the modal ("POPULAR CATEGORIES", "RECENT SEARCHES") starts with an animated cyan dot.
  • Same animation as the IQ "Por qué Startoner" section — apexEyebrowPulse 2.4s cubic-bezier(.4,0,.6,1) infinite.
  • Label typography: 11px / 700 / letter-spacing 0.16em / uppercase / opacity 0.45.

Popular-category chips

  • CMYK rotation via nth-child(4n+N) — first chip cyan, second magenta, third yellow, fourth mint, repeat.
  • Hover: bg rgba(var(--chip-rgb), 0.10) + border rgba(var(--chip-rgb), 0.42) + glow 0 0 18px rgba(var(--chip-rgb), 0.22).

  • Vendor: 10px / uppercase / 0.12em letter-spacing / opacity 0.42.

  • When an item has no image or featured_image, render an SVG icon instead of an empty box.

16

Glows: when silver, when CMYK.

The brand has two rotating-halo signatures. They are NOT interchangeable. The choice depends on the CTA's role on the page, not on taste. Mixing them on the same page produces visual noise; mixing them on the same component produces semantic confusion. A premium page reserves CMYK for the primary money action.

Primary money actions.

"Buy / talk to sales / subscribe." Celebration energy. Communicates "this is where you want to go."

Where it's used:

Golden rule: If in doubt, use silver. The brand reads more sophisticated when CMYK is reserved for the page's primary CTA. A page with two CMYK halos competes with itself; a page with two silvers reads cohesive.
Mandatory halo geometry: inset ≥ -6px (idle) and ≥ -10px (hover). filter: blur(...) ≥ 10px. Without these parameters, the bright stop of the gradient renders as a static line (slash) on one side of the CTA. Lesson from session 2026-04-28: the first apex-search v3 failed with inset: -1px and no blur — visible artifact.

Neon underline with tapered ends.

Footer hover effect (`.ft-menu a::after`, `.ft-legal-links a::after`). A CMYK trace with pointed ends instead of straight edges — visual reference: Kiwi.com. The cigar/leaf shape communicates "edited destination, not UI rectangle". The drop-shadow glow follows the tapered silhouette, NOT the rectangular bounding box.

Shape — clip-path polygon

A 10-vertex polygon, symmetric top/bottom and left/right. More vertices = smoother curve. Fewer = more pointy.

clip-path: polygon(
  0% 50%,
  4% 25%, 10% 5%,
  90% 5%, 96% 25%,
  100% 50%,
  96% 75%, 90% 95%,
  10% 95%, 4% 75%
);

For legal-links (thinner line, smaller text), use more aggressive transitions — 5%, 12% instead of 4%, 10% — to keep the tapered silhouette visible at reduced size.

Glow — drop-shadow, NOT box-shadow

Critical: box-shadow follows the rectangular bounding box and creates a square halo around a tapered shape. It will break the illusion.

filter: drop-shadow() follows the element's alpha channel, which clip-path modifies. The halo conforms to the curve.

filter:
  drop-shadow(0 0 4px rgba(var(--cyan), 0.65))
  drop-shadow(0 0 10px rgba(var(--mint), 0.32));

Color — cyan→mint on dark, ink-blue→mint-green on light

  • Dark theme: cyan gradient rgb(var(--ft-cyan)) → mint rgb(var(--ft-mint)).
  • Light theme: #00b4d8 → #2d9c5e (saturated, not full-neon — neon disappears against light backgrounds).
  • The horizontal gradient makes the left half of the line read cyan and the right half read mint, giving it motion even when the animation isn't running.

Animation — width 0 → 100%, premium easing

  • transition: width .38s cubic-bezier(.16, 1, .3, 1).
  • Same easing as the hero reveal — feels like a native piece.
  • The underline draws left-to-right on hover. Because clip-path is proportional to the element width, the cigar shape always reads correctly regardless of completion %.

What we learned this week.

Three new anti-patterns discovered during the April 28, 2026 sprint. Each cost at least one round of "this looks broken, fix it" before being identified as a rule. Codified here so they don't happen again.

CMYK conic-gradient with only 3 stops.

Cyan→magenta→yellow→cyan creates a visible 3-color pinwheel with abrupt transitions. The eye reads 3 distinct regions, not a rainbow. Fix: add mint as the fourth color + cyan loop = 5 stops total. The gap between stops drops from 120° to 90° and the gradient reads continuous under blur. Lesson: v1 of the "View all results" CTA in the search modal failed on this.

Absolute-corner badge over titles that can wrap.

A position: absolute; top: 8px; right: 8px; on a card with a 2-line title inevitably overlaps the last word of the title. Visible: "ISO 9001" obscured by "OLECCIÓN". Fix: restructure to an inline meta-row above the title containing vendor + badge inline with display: flex. Predictable, never overlaps, semantically cleaner.

Semi-translucent background under a rotating gradient.

A CTA with background: rgba(18, 18, 22, 0.85) and a ::before using conic-gradient lets the gradient bleed through the button face — visible color bands inside the pill. Fix: solid background (#121216) or the hero wrapper-pattern. Transparency + halo is a visual trap.
